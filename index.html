Unity and CppUTest are great. Writing the same patterns over and over while the hardware team changes the HAL again is not.

Manual test writing is slow and repetitive

Every new driver or module means another file full of nearly identical setup, boundary cases, and stub declarations.

Real firmware touches registers, SPI, I2C, and timers. You either link the whole HAL or spend hours writing fakes that drift from reality.

Frequently asked questions

What test frameworks does it support?
Unity is the primary, fully supported target. CppUTest and Google Test formatters are also included.
Does it work air-gapped?
Yes. After the one-time license activation (or during the 30-day trial), everything runs 100% locally.
What platforms and compilers are supported?
Tested on Windows and Ubuntu with gcc. You can enter a custom compile command for other compilers. Additional native compiler support is available through customer-funded development.
